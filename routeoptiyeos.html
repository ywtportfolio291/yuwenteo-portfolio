Vending Machine Route Optimisation for Yeo’s Pte Ltd

I was the Power BI Developer in a cross-functional team tasked with improving operational efficiency for a local vending machine distributor. Through iterative dashboard development and biweekly client review meetings, I focused on turning raw data into user-friendly visuals that informed smarter scheduling decisions and cost reductions.

The Challenge

A look at the vending machines at the heart of Yeo’s nationwide servicing operations.

Yeo’s operates over 1000 vending machines across Singapore, divided into refill and sales collection routes managed by individual servicemen. These routes were built organically over time, based on familiarity rather than logic, resulting in:

  • Uneven workload distribution across servicemen
  • No standardised scheduling system to guide sales collection
  • High operational costs from inefficiencies

We set out to answer: “How might we use data to optimise route assignments, structure collection schedules, and surface operational insights — without depending on costly logistics-specific platforms?”

User Insights

Our team during a field visit with one of Yeo’s servicemen — a key part of our stakeholder immersion process.

We began by speaking with Yeo’s operations lead and shadowing a few servicemen in the field to understand their current routing practices and pain points.

These conversations revealed four key insights:

Insight What We Learned
Servicemen planned their routes manually each day Servicemen followed routes based on experience and habit, leading to inconsistent refill patterns and imbalanced coverage
Unequal workloads led to fatigue and inefficiency Some servicemen were assigned nearly twice the number of machines as others.
Lack of visibility for stakeholders Performance tracking existed only in Excel spreadsheets — limiting operational oversight.

Our Approach

These findings shaped our direction: rather than automating everything, we focused on building tools that support both frontline staff and operational decision-makers.

We developed two complementary systems:

  • Power BI Dashboard – to visualise sales, servicing effort, and cluster performance
  • Scheduling Engine – to generate practical, workload-aware servicing plans

Together, these solutions aimed to reduce inefficiencies while respecting the servicemen’s on-ground experience.

Power BI Dashboard

The original goal for this dashboard was to help senior management understand overall vending machine sales performance in 2022 — with a focus on revenue trends and sales per route.

However, we saw an opportunity to go further.

Elevating the Scope

Rather than stopping at descriptive analytics, we expanded the dashboard into a strategic planning tool by integrating:

  • Route comparisons between legacy and optimised clusters
  • Cost-to-time efficiency analysis, combining sales data with travel and scheduling factors
  • ROI analysis, highlighting machines with low returns relative to servicing effort (time & operational cost)

This shift turned the dashboard from a static report into an interactive decision-support system — one that could answer not just “What happened?” but also “What should we do next?”

Schedule Generator

Servicemen at Yeo’s were responsible for restocking vending machines across their assigned clusters.

While they were familiar with their routes, daily planning was:

  • Entirely manual
  • Time-consuming
  • Physically taxing — especially in environments like schools, where servicemen navigated multiple floors and made repeated trips between the vending machines and their truck.

To ease this operational burden, we built a Python-based linear optimiser using PuLP that generates a 25-day rotating schedule per cluster — intelligently accounting for:

  • Machine priority (based on sales tiers)
  • Accessibility tags (e.g., “School”, “Remote”)
  • Estimated servicing effort and time constraints

Optimisation Logic

Feature Logic
Sales-Based Prioritisation Machines were grouped into High, Medium, and Low sales tiers — each assigned different visit frequencies
Accessibility Tagging Location tags like School, Remote, or City Centre influenced estimated servicing effort per machine
Daily Workload Limits Each day's schedule capped by machine count or time budget to prevent fatigue
Even Visit Distribution Visit frequency was spaced evenly across the month to avoid front/backloading

The schedule generator transformed manual planning into a structured, optimised workflow — reducing servicemen’s burden while enabling managers to compare costs and improve efficiency across clusters.

Impact

Final showcase of our project

This project empowered Yeo’s to move from intuition-based operations to data-driven route optimisation — enabling clearer decision-making, fairer workload distribution, and smarter use of servicing resources.

From high-level dashboards to daily schedules, every layer of the solution was designed to improve transparency, operational efficiency, and long-term scalability.

Learnings

While I focused primarily on designing and building the Power BI dashboard, revisiting it later — with feedback from my professors and industry mentors — revealed a key lesson:

I should have invested more time upfront in low-fidelity prototyping to plan the layout, prioritise information flow, and reduce visual clutter.

Initially, I was too focused on maximising screen space, which led to including charts that didn’t meaningfully support the narrative. This made the dashboard harder to digest and required multiple iterations to refine.

If I were to redo the project, I would begin with a lo-fi wireframe focused on:

  • Clear visual hierarchy
  • Primary KPIs front and center
  • Supporting charts only where they drive decision-making
  • Grouped filters and controls for easier usability

This approach would have saved time, improved storytelling, and made the dashboard more intuitive from the start.

Future Work

While our current solution successfully streamlined planning, scheduling, and performance reporting, there are several directions we would have explored further with more time and resources:

Mobile Access for Servicemen

Convert the Excel-based schedules into a mobile-accessible app or web interface, allowing servicemen to:

Mobile Application for Servicemen

  • View their daily schedule on the go
  • Check off completed machines in real time
  • Submit feedback on accessibility or machine status

This would improve day-to-day usability and create a tighter feedback loop between ground operations and the scheduling system.

Dynamic Route Reassignment with Traffic Awareness

Extend the clustering and scheduling engine to handle dynamic rebalancing when:

  • A serviceman is unavailable (e.g., leave or emergency)
  • Sales volumes shift significantly over time
  • Traffic disruptions occur (e.g., road closures, accidents, or jams)

By integrating live traffic data, the system could automatically redirect servicemen to alternative machines — minimising idle time, improving efficiency, and keeping route execution adaptable to real-world conditions.
